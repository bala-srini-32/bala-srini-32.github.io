Research focus

Building the infrastructure beneath agentic AI.

My work sits at the platform layer: the primitives that let builders compose, secure, customize, search over, and evaluate populations of LLM agents in production.

01

Agentic security & governance

Scanning, red-teaming, and indexing composite agentic systems (LLM agents, MCP servers, tools, and skill files) before they ship, with findings mapped to the OWASP Agentic AI Top-10.

02

Agentic Customization & optimization

Mining OpenTelemetry traces from live agent runs to auto-generate skill files, tighten system prompts and tool descriptions, and train reinforcement-learning policies that decide which tools to run sequentially vs. in parallel.

03

Routing, eval & alignment

Intelligent model routing with calibrated quality predictors and user-controlled cost/latency tradeoffs; automatic prompt optimization; trajectory evaluation and safety adherence for composite agents.

04

Symmetry Aware representation learning

Work on Invariant and equivariant architectures for baking in discrete and continuous symmetries into neural networks: Janossy Pooling, Relational Pooling, equivalence of positional and structural embeddings, and ESAN subgraph GNNs.

  4. Unified framework for automatic prompt optimization spanning iteration depth, filtering strategies, prompt generation, and feedback mechanisms. Defines formal APO problem formulations and identifies axes of meaningful advancement.

  5. Multi-task fine-tuning approach for related table discovery in data lakes, enabling LLMs to jointly reason over schema, content, and provenance signals to find semantically related tables at enterprise scale.

  6. Selects representative in-context examples by building semantic graphs over unlabeled candidates and solving a maximum-coverage problem, augmented with LLM uncertainty. Beats baselines by 2 to 4.6% across ten datasets and seven LLMs with 2× better annotation-budget efficiency.

    PDFACL →

  7. Score-based diffusion in a VAE-learned latent space for mixed-type tabular data. Cuts column distribution and pair-wise correlation estimation error by 86% and 67% vs. competitive baselines.

    PDFarXiv →

  8. Parameter-efficient framework using knowledge graphs to learn transformations between independently trained biomedical foundation models without fine-tuning. Outperforms KG embedding baselines by ~76% on cross-modal retrieval and enables multimodal biomedical question answering.

    PDFarXiv →

  9. Advances LLMs as open-domain table reasoners by combining retrieval-augmented evidence gathering with iterative chain-of-thought reasoning over tables, enabling strong performance on complex table QA tasks without task-specific fine-tuning.

    arXiv →

  10. Captures tabular invariances with hypergraphs: cells become nodes, row/column/table co-occurrences become hyperedges. Maximally invariant under natural table permutations; consistently outperforms baselines on four downstream tasks with minimal pretraining.

    PDFarXiv →

  11. Frames abbreviated column-name expansion as conditional natural-language generation on 384K synthetic and 9.2K human-annotated pairs. Conditioning LLMs on table content and headers resolves polysemy and matches human performance on real-world table schemas.

    PDFarXiv →

  12. Addresses GNN expressiveness limits by representing each graph as a set of subgraphs processed with equivariant architectures. Proves strictly higher expressiveness via WL variants, introduces tractable subgraph-sampling, and improves both theory and empirical performance.

    PDFarXiv →

  13. Unifies positional and structural node embeddings under a common theoretical framework, proving their equivalence under mild conditions and providing a principled basis for combining both approaches.

    PDFarXiv →

  14. Introduces Relational Pooling via random injective mappings, achieving maximum expressiveness under permutation invariance. Generalizes the Weisfeiler-Lehman test and many popular GNN architectures within a single framework.

    PDFarXiv →

  15. Expresses permutation-invariant functions of sequences as the average of a permutation-sensitive function over all reorderings, unlocking the full literature of RNNs and CNNs for invariant tasks. Introduces tractable approximations via canonical orderings, k-ary interactions, and stochastic optimization.
